# ABSTRACT

This study investigates the fatigue behavior of an ultra-large 15 MW semi-submersible floating offshore wind turbine (FOWT) under various transient scenarios, including startup, shutdown, and fault conditions, which are often overlooked in conventional analyses. A high-fidelity simulation framework was developed, integrating TurbSim for three-dimensional wind field, OpenFAST for aero-hydro-servo-elastic load simulation, and MLife for fatigue assessment via damage equivalent load (DEL). The results show that platform motion has a negligible impact on power output but significantly affects structural responses. Compared to fixed platforms, moving configurations reduce blade-root fatigue by up to $41.83\%$ during rotor idling and tower-base fatigue by up to $3.29\%$ under fault-induced shutdowns. In contrast, shutdown transients can amplify tower-base shear and bending DEL by up to $54.16\%$ and $62.26\%$ , respectively, especially at high pitch rates. Overall, moving platforms demonstrate superior fatigue mitigation and more uniform load distribution, particularly during abrupt shutdowns or rotor imbalance.

# ABSTRACT

This paper introduces a continuous wavelet transform-based method for identifying aerodynamic damping in floating offshore wind turbines (FOWTs) with large yaw angles, addressing complex aeroelastic instability under extreme environmental conditions. The method systematically analyzes time-varying aerodynamic damping matrices for FOWT blades under stochastic wind and wave loads. Key findings reveal that under extreme loads, edgewise and flapwise damping coefficients transition from weak to strong negative damping as wind speed increases, driving aeroelastic instability. In contrast, off-diagonal damping coefficients exhibit significant positive damping, highlighting the complexity of multi-degree-of-freedom coupled excitation. Surge and pitch motions significantly influence edgewise damping through coupling coefficients, whereas heave damping exhibits an inverse sensitivity to wind speed. A comprehensive system analysis confirms the method's robustness, maintaining prediction accuracy within $10\%$ even under extreme divergence, thus validating its reliability for engineering applications.
