# ABSTRACT

The wake effect in offshore wind farms significantly reduces power output. Extensive studies have employed active wake control (AWC) and layout optimization for mitigation. This study proposes a novel approach using the synergistic effect of small vertical axis wind turbine array (VAWTA) with large horizontal axis wind turbines (HAWTs) to promote breaking blade tip vortex barriers and accelerate wake recovery. Blade-resolved computational fluid dynamics (CFD) simulation under fixed-bottom configurations evaluates two tandem NREL 5 MW HAWTs, analyzing VAWTA layout and array parameter impacts on power output, aerodynamic performance and wake recovery. The findings indicate that great power generation occurs at 6D distance between VAWTA and downstream HAWT. A suitable relative height between the highest point of the VAWT impeller and the lowest point of the HAWT impeller can effectively enhance the mixing of high and low-speed flow in the wake region. Additionally, outward-counter-rotating (OCR) VAWTA configuration splits the incoming flow and effectively expands the wake area. A parabolic relationship between VAWTA distance and HAWT2 power output is further revealed, and the optimal distance yields a $20\%$ increase in VSWF power output. This approach can provide novel insights for newly built and in-service offshore wind farms.
